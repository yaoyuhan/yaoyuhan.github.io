About Me

I'm Yuhan Yao (姚雨含), a second-year graduate student in Astronomy at Caltech. My advisor is Shri Kulkarni.

I work on the Zwicky Transient Facility (ZTF) to study supernova explosions and stellar evolution. Using the high-cadence experiment undertook by ZTF, I constructed a large uniform dataset of early observations of Type Ia supernovae (Yao et al. 2019b).

I obtained my B.S. in Astronomy at Peking University. As an undergraduate, I worked with the LAMOST stellar group to study Mira stars (Yao et al. 2017), under the instruction of Chao Liu, Licai Deng, and Richard de Grijs. I did my undergrad thesis to understand the evolution of primordial disks (Yao et al. 2018) at the University of Michigan, where my mentor was Michael Meyer. During my senior year, I also spent some time at Tsinghua University, working with Hua Feng on building a disk-wind irradiation model to explain the optical SED of ultraluminous X-ray sources (Yao et al. 2019a).
